The Lab for the Analysis of Zombie Activity and Research into Undead Simulations (LAZARUS) was founded at Texas Tech University in 2014 as the result of the synergistic efforts of the Department of Mathematics & Statistics, the Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Center for Outreach, Research & Education (STEM-CORE), and a number of independent initiatives funded by the National Science Foundation. The lab is designed to serve two purposes: (1) support research opportunities for undergraduate and graduate students, and (2) support K12 outreach efforts designed to increase awareness and interest in STEM academic and career pathways.
